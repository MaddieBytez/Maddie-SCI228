Course Information

Name: Nutrition, Health & Wellness w/Lab

Code: SCI228

Description: This course provides an overview of basic nutrients the body requires for health and life, and dispels common nutrition myths. The role of nutrition in various biological phases of the human life cycle, as well as psychological and sociological implications of food, are discussed. Students also learn how the scientific method of inquiry is used in the nutritional science and health fields. In the lab, students collect observational data, employ computer simulations, and prepare and sample various foods.

Prerequisites: N/A

Course Objectives

  • Week 1: Discuss the impact of food choices on a person’s overall health.
  • Week 2: Assess the healthfulness of one's diet using food guides and food labels, and discuss how the human body digests, absorbs, transports, and utilizes nutrients in the diet.
  • Week 3: Describe the structure and functions of the energy-yielding nutrients (carbohydrates, fats, and protein) and discuss their roles in relation to health and disease.
  • Week 4: Discuss the role of vitamins, minerals, and water in maintaining good health.
  • Week 5: Describe the principles of a sound fitness program, and discuss the factors involved in maintaining a healthy body weight and the effect of nutrition on performance.
  • Week 6: Discuss the nutrient needs and challenges specific to the various life cycles from prenatal, during lactation, infancy, toddler, childhood, adulthood, and older adulthood.
  • Week 7: Assess good food safety practices, and understand factors that will affect the future of the food supply and sustainability.
  • Week 8: Final Exam
